BMC's Evans started the 42.5 km race in Grenoble against the clock with a 57 seconds deficit to Andy Schleck in the race for the yellow jersey, but easily overhauled the Luxembourg rider as he finished in second place behind 20th stage winner Tony Martin of Germany. Evans, the 2009 world champion, will go into Sunday's concluding stage to Paris with a one minute 34 second lead over Andy Schleck, whose older brother Frank Schleck will finish third overall as he trails by two minutes and 30 seconds.
